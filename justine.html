Key Areas of Gender Inequality

1. Education

In some parts of the world, girls are less likely than boys to attend school.

Cultural, economic, or social barriers often hinder women's access to quality education.

2. Employment

Women frequently earn less than men for the same work (gender pay gap).

They are often underrepresented in leadership roles and face discrimination in hiring and promotions.

3. Healthcare

In some regions, women lack access to essential healthcare services, particularly reproductive and maternal care.

Gender biases in medical research and treatment can lead to poorer outcomes for women.

4. Political Representation

Women hold fewer political positions and are underrepresented in decision-making roles globally.

This limits the focus on policies that address gender-specific issues.

5. Violence and Safety

Women are more likely to experience domestic violence, sexual harassment, and gender-based violence.

In many societies, legal systems do not adequately protect women from these abuses.

6. Cultural Norms and Practices

Traditional gender roles and stereotypes perpetuate inequalities.

Practices like child marriage, female genital mutilation, and honor-based violence disproportionately affect women.
